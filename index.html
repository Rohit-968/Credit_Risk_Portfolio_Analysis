Key Underwriting Findings & Portfolio Thesis
  • Late payment history is the dominant driver of risk classification. Customers with 4+ late payments are flagged high-risk in ~97% of cases, compared to ~53% for customers with 0-3 late payments.
  • Credit score remains the strongest underwriting signal: the 'Poor' band (<580) is almost universally high-risk (99.5%), while bands 580+ show comparable ~53-57% risk rates, suggesting a binary cut is optimal.
  • Risk rates are broadly stable across demographics: segmentation should prioritize behavioral/payment signals (late payments, scores) over age and employment status.
  • Fraud incidence (2.9% overall) is concentrated entirely within the high-risk segment, reinforcing late payments and credit score as compounding underwriting red flags.
